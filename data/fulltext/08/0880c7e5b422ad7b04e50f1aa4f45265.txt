Background: Previous studies indicate that a postbiotic extract from 
Aquaphilus dolomiae (ADE-G3) improves skin barrier function and 
relieves neuroinflammation. Objectives: Evaluation of an ADE-G3-
based soothing cream for managing sensitive facial skin. Materials & 
Methods: This real-world, international, pre-post comparative study 
involved adults with sensitive facial skin who used the study prod-
uct once or twice daily for two to three months according to usual 
practice. Subjects reported changes in perceived clinical symptoms 
using self-administered questionnaires. Physicians assessed changes 
in xerosis severity, overall product effectiveness and tolerability. User 
satisfaction and quality of life (QoL) assessments, and subgroup 
analyses according to the factors triggering sensitive skin were also 
conducted. Results: In total, 2,382 subjects with sensitive facial skin 
(female: 79%; median age: 40 years) were included. An immediate 
skin soothing effect after the first ADE-G3-based cream application 
was reported by 93% of subjects, and improvements in symptoms 
were reported in 94% after a mean of nine days of product use. 
After several months of use (mean: 71±21 days), xerosis severity 
and dermatological-related QoL significantly improved in the whole 
study population and in the subgroups (p<0.001). At the end of the 
study, 92% of users were satisfied with the product and 95% reported 
improvements in their overall skin condition. Physicians found the 
cream to be effective and well tolerated in 92% and 98% of subjects, 
respectively. Conclusion: Regular use of the ADE-G3-based cream 
was shown to be effective in real-world management of sensitive facial 
skin, regardless of the factors involved in triggering skin sensitivity.

ensitive or reactive skin is a common dermatolog-
ical problem in the general population, with prev-
alence estimates from self-reporting surveys 
indicating that approximately 60% to 70% of women and 
50% to 60% of men are affected [1]. 
Sensitive skin is a complaint with a highly subjective 
component, which makes its diagnosis and treatment 
very difficult. Indeed, there is currently no standard diag-
nostic test for the objective identification of this skin 
syndrome, thus diagnosis mainly relies on self-assess-
ment questionnaires [2-4]. Symptoms reported by 
subjects mainly include burning sensations, pruritus, 
tingling, skin redness and pain, which can be accompa-
nied by other clinical signs such as mild erythema, 
xerosis, urticaria, telangiectasias or desquamation. 
However, in most cases, there are only subjective symp-
toms. The symptoms are not related to lesions attribut-
able to any other skin disease and occur in response to 
stimuli that do not normally produce such effects [4, 5].
The syndrome of sensitive skin profoundly impacts 
quality of life (QoL) of the individuals affected, particu-
larly when the symptoms and visible consequences of 
the condition -such as skin redness, scaling or swelling- 
manifest on the face.
Various factors can aggravate the symptoms of sensitive 
skin or initiate cutaneous reactions including: environ-
mental factors, such as exposure to ultraviolet rays or 
sunlight, air pollution, heat, cold and wind; factors asso-
ciated with lifestyle such as the use of cosmetics, a spicy 
diet, the consumption of alcohol or smoking; and phys-
iological factors, such as stress or hormones [1, 4]. Some 
pathologies, medical treatments or dermatological 
procedures can also exacerbate sensitive skin. This skin 
condition thus appears to be complex, and although not 
all the pathophysiological mechanisms have been clearly 
established, sensitive skin is increasingly being recog-
nized as a neurodermatological syndrome. Current data 
indeed indicate that individuals with sensitive skin

As part of its research on the microbiota derived from 
Avène thermal spring water (France) and on the patho-

active ingredients. Among these, the extract ADE-G3 
(DSensinoseTM) derived from the bacterium, Aquaphilus

genic skin inflammation [6] and on skin barrier function 
[7]. More specifically, in vitro studies have indicated that 
this postbiotic may act on several nociceptors and 
inflammation amplification loops, and thereby may 
decrease cutaneous sensitivity via an anaesthetic-like 
mechanism [6]. Several soothing formulations (cream 
and balm) incorporating the postbiotic ADE-G3 have 
been designed for the management of  sensitive facial 
skin, and have demonstrated immediate and long-term 
effects on the reduction of skin sensitivity in clinical eval-
uations [8]. Thus, taken together, the in vitro and clinical 
data indicate that the relieving effects of ADE-G3-
containing products are likely due to the mitigation of 
the cutaneous neurosensory response associated with 
this syndrome.
The current study was conducted to investigate real-
world effectiveness and tolerability of an ADE-G3-based 
cream in a large international cohort of subjects with 
sensitive facial skin. This study included evaluation of 
the immediate and long-term effects of the study product 
on the clinical symptoms of sensitive skin, as well as 
assessment of the changes in severity of xerosis after 
several months of product use. The impact on QoL, and 
overall user satisfaction, product effectiveness and toler-
ability were also assessed.

Participants
The sponsor invited physicians from 520 medical centres 
to participate in the study. Those agreeing to participate 
were asked to recruit at least five subjects for whom use 
of the study product was advised, i.e., adults with skin 
prone to irritation (sensitive, reactive, or hyper-reactive 
skin), skin susceptible to allergens (intolerant skin or 
skin prone to allergic contact dermatitis), or skin irrita-
tion due to dermatological pathologies or previously 
performed dermatological procedures, and whose facial 
skin type was rated by the physician as dry, normal or 
a combination of both. Subjects with oily skin, a skin 
disease affecting the face, and those who were intolerant 
to one of the components of the product were not eligible 
for product use. Moreover, subjects using a drug treat-
ment that could interfere with the evaluation of the 
results were not included.
Study product
The study product was a commercially available dermo-
cosmetic: Tolérance Control® soothing cream (Avène, 
France) containing the postbiotic ADE-G3 extract 
(D-SensinoseTM) as the main non-medicated active 
ingredient, as well as Avène thermal spring water, 
caprylic/capric triglyceride, ethylhexyl palmitate, glyc-
erin, cetearyl alcohol, squalane, Cetearyl glucoside, cetyl 
esters, arginine, beeswax (Cera alba), citric acid, trometh-
amine, water (aqua) and xanthan gum.
Materials and methods
Study design and ethics
This real-world, prospective, observational, multicentre, 
pre-post comparative study was conducted from 1st 
September 2021 to 30th November 2022 in 13 countries 
(Australia, Belgium, Czech Republic, Germany, France, 
Greece, Italy, Japan, Poland, Portugal, Romania, 
Slovakia, and the United States). The study involved 
two visits: an inclusion visit (Visit 1), and a follow-up 
visit (Visit 2) after about two to three months of product 
use. These visits were part of the routine follow-up of 
the included subjects by the physician who was usually 
responsible for their dermatological management. 
This study was performed in accordance with the 
Helsinki Declaration of 1964 and its later amendments, 
and complied with good clinical practice guidelines 
(CPMP/ICH/135/95, https://www.ema.europa.eu) and 
current legislation for clinical trials involving cosmetics 
in the countries where the study was conducted

At inclusion, the physicians collected demographic and 
clinical data from the subjects with sensitive skin, 
including Fitzpatrick’s phototype and facial skin type.

during the three days prior to cream application 
(including perceived and visible manifestations) were also 
recorded at baseline through a multiple-choice self-as-

Tolerability
Tolerability was ranked by the physician at Visit 2 using 
a 4-point scale from 0 (poor) to 3 (very good). The 
subjects also reported any acceptability issues they 
encountered when using the product.
Statistical analyses
Descriptive data were expressed as the mean, standard 
deviation (SD) and range (min-max), or as the number 
and percentage (N and %) for each variable, as appro-
priate. Between-visit differences in the means were 
expressed as absolute changes (initial value-final value) 
and as relative changes (initial value-final value/initial 
value*100).
Changes from baseline in the SS-10 score, xerosis severity 
score and DLQI score were analysed in the whole study 
population and in the three subpopulations of subjects 
grouped according to types of factors contributing to 
skin sensitivity reported at baseline, using either the 
parametric Student’s t-test or the non-parametric 
Wilcoxon test. A p value of less than 0.05 was considered 
as statistically significant. All statistical analyses were 
carried out using SAS®, version 9.4.
Evaluation criteria
The main evaluation criterion was the effectiveness of 
the study product on the clinical symptoms of sensitive 
skin on the face in the whole study population, immedi-
ately after the first application, and after two to three 
months of use in a real-life setting. Secondary criteria 
included changes between baseline and follow-up in 
xerosis severity, as assessed by the physician, and in QoL 
and dermatological-related QoL, as assessed by the 
subjects. These criteria were also analysed in three 
subpopulations of subjects grouped according to the 
types of factors contributing to skin sensitivity reported 
at baseline. Additional outcomes included time to 
improvement of symptoms, global product effectiveness 
and user satisfaction, and product tolerability.
Assessment methods
Clinical symptoms and signs
Users assessed the clinical symptoms of sensitive facial 
skin at baseline before product application, 30 seconds 
after the first application (Visit 1) and at follow-up 
(Visit 2) using the optimized and validated Sensitive 
Scale-10 (SS-10), comprising 10 items each rated on a 
10-point visual analogue scale (VAS) to measure severity 
of skin sensitivity [3]. On this scale, the highest possible 
total score is 100, and a score above 20 identifies indi-
viduals with sensitive skin. Users were also asked to 
estimate the time to improvement of their symptoms at 
Visit 2. Xerosis severity on the face was assessed at base-
line (before product use) and at follow-up by the physi-
cian using a 10-point VAS ranging from 0 (no skin 
dryness) to 10 (severe skin dryness).

Overall user satisfaction and product effectiveness
Most users were “satisfied” or “very satisfied” with the 
immediate effect of the cream, according to the subject 
(90%) and the physician (93%) assessments (figure 4A, 
supplementary table 2). At follow-up, the cumulated 
percentage of “satisfied” and “very satisfied” subjects 
increased to 92% and 96%, respectively. The vast 
majority of subjects (95%, 2,233/2,344) also found that 
the regular use of the cream led to an overall improve-
ment in their skin sensitivity. According to the physician, 
the signs and symptoms of sensitive skin improved signif-
icantly or even completely resolved in 92% (1,849/2,020) 
of subjects at the end of the study (figure 4B, supplemen-
tary table 3). The overall level of product effectiveness 
was similar regardless of the contributing factors 
reported at baseline, with symptoms improving or 
resolving completely in 91% to 94% of subjects across 
the subgroups.
Study product use
Almost all the included subjects (95%) used the product 
as advised, with 77% (1817/2362) of them reporting that 
they applied the cream twice a day, and 22% (516/2362) 
reporting that they applied it once a day. The mean dura-
tion of product use was 71±21 days (based on data from 
2,264 subjects).
Product effectiveness on clinical symptoms and 
signs 
The first application of the ADE-G3-based cream 
resulted in immediate soothing of the skin in 93% of 
subjects (1911/2,064). Statistically significant improve-
ments in the clinical symptoms of sensitive facial skin 
were also reported in the whole study population, with 
the mean SS-10 score decreasing by –55% between base-
line and 30 seconds after product application (p<0.001), 
and by –79% between baseline and follow-up after two 
to three months of use (p<0.001) (figure 2A). Compared 
to baseline, mean scores for the 10 individual question-
naire items were also significantly lower immediately after 
application and at follow-up (supplementary table 1). 
 
Furthermore, the regular use of the cream led to a reduc-
tion in xerosis severity, with a significant decrease in 
mean severity score between baseline and follow-up of 
–72% (p<0.001) (figure 2B, supplementary table 1). 
Similar decreases in SS-10 and xerosis severity scores 
between baseline and follow-up were observed in all 
three of the contributing factor subgroups, with changes 
of –79% to –81% for SS-10 scores and of –72% to –74% 
for xerosis severity across all groups (p<0.001 for all 
analyses) (figure 2A, B, supplementary table 1). Overall, 
most subjects (94%, 2,229/2,382) reported that their 
symptoms improved after a mean time of product use 
of nine days (supplementary table 2).
Tolerability
Product tolerability was rated by the physicians as 
“good” or “very good” in 98% (2,276/2,329) of subjects 
at follow-up (supplementary table 2). Only one case 
reported an undesirable event in the cosmetovigilance 
database over the study period.
Discussion
This multicentre, pre–post comparative study, conducted 
on a large cohort of subjects with sensitive facial skin, 
demonstrated that the daily use of an ADE-G3-based 
cream in real-life conditions led to statistically significant 
decreases in all the visible and perceived symptoms 
assessed. The study product also drastically reduced the 
severity of xerosis after two to three months of product 
use, and improvements in QoL, with a notable impact 
on sensitive skin-related sleep disturbances and stress, 
were also reported. Furthermore, physicians reported 
that the product was effective overall for improving 
sensitive skin in a high proportion of subjects, and rated 
the tolerability of the cream as good or very good in most 
subjects. Importantly, improvements in the symptoms 
assessed were also reported immediately after use, and 
regardless of the type of contributing factors initially 
reported to trigger skin sensitivity.

Strategies for the treatment of sensitive skin mainly rely 
on restoration of the skin barrier and reducing inflam-
mation [2]. As subjects with sensitive skin have a thinner 
and more permeable epidermis than individuals with 
other skin types, it is essential to restore and maintain 
the integrity of this protective skin layer. However, as 
skin sensitivity is known to involve alterations in cuta-
neous neurological components, such as higher levels of 
nerve growth factor in the epidermis and increased sensi-
tivity to electrical stimuli, topical products acting on 
sensory neurons or on neuromediators are particularly 
relevant for helping to alleviate hyperalgesia and other 
unpleasant sensations associated with sensitive skin. An 
increasing number of novel products that can mitigate
neuronal skin sensitivity and neurogenic inflammation, 
in addition to hydrating and protecting the skin, have 
recently been developed to offer an integral solution to 
subjects with sensitive skin [4]. 
Several actives that can modulate neurogenic targets 
have been identified and incorporated into dermocos-
metic products for sensitive skin, but few studies 
comparing the effectiveness of these actives in topical 
formulations have been published. For instance, neuro-
sensine, also known as acetyl dipeptide-1 cetyl ester, has 
been shown to lower the hyperexcitability of nerve 
endings by stimulating the production of endorphins and 
enkephalins in keratinocytes and is used in dermocos-
metics to reduce burning sensations and irritation asso-
ciated with sensitive skin [17, 18]. Products containing 
4-t-butylcyclohexanol have also been reported to have 
soothing and calming effects on the skin by inhibiting

Postbiotics are particularly promising naturally-derived 
actives with multiple relevant properties for dermocos-
metics [23]. The postbiotic ADE-G3-based soothing
cream evaluated in our real-life study showed good 
effectiveness on sensitive skin, which is likely related to 
the combined action of the postbiotic for reducing the 
neurogenic inflammation associated with skin sensitivity 
and other product ingredients for improving skin hydra-
tion and skin barrier function. The postbiotic ADE-G3 
has indeed been shown to reduce neurogenic inflamma-
tion in in vitro models through several effects, including 
inhibition of the release of inflammatory cytokines and 
interleukins by keratinocytes and the release of substance 
P (SP) by sensory neurons, as well as a decrease in 
SP-stimulated degranulation of mast cells [6]. The effec-
tiveness of dermocosmetics containing this innovative

and natural active to alleviate unpleasant sensory effects 
has also been assessed in subjects with sensitive or reac-
tive skin. In initial clinical studies using quantitative 
measurements, the cream and balm formulations 
containing this ingredient were shown to not only 
protect the skin from dehydration and improve skin 
barrier integrity by reducing transepithelial water loss 
(TEWL) and increasing the lipid index [7, 24], but also 
to attenuate the neurosensory response associated with 
sensitive skin by reducing the electrodermal response 
following heat and friction stimulation [8]. Moreover, 
the formulations were designed to mimic the structure 
and composition of the skin by incorporating multiple 
lipid and non-lipid components to help reinforce the 
lipid barrier and promote uniform spreading and 
improved penetration of the product [24]. These prod-
ucts also use Avène thermal spring water, with intrinsic 
soothing and anti-irritant properties [25, 26], as a vehicle 
and are therefore very well tolerated and particularly 
suitable for use on sensitive skin. Furthermore, it should 
be noted that the products are packaged in Sterile 
Cosmetic® tubes, which protect the formula from the 
air and bacteria and prevent degradation of ingredients 
without the need to add preservatives, both of which 
may worsen skin symptoms [2].
Overall, the available data from the current study and 
previously published reports show that ADE-G3-based 
topical products offer an effective and safe solution for 
immediate and long-term relief of symptoms in subjects 
suffering from sensitive skin, regardless of whether the 
factors contributing to the skin sensitivity are endoge-
nous (emotional factors or disease), environmental 
(weather, pollution or solar exposure), related to lifestyle 
(diet, use of cosmetics) or associated with dermatological 
treatments or procedures. These moisturizers can there-
fore be used as part of a daily skincare routine to soothe 
sensitive skin, maintain skin barrier function, relieve 
pain and discomfort, and prevent the occurrence of cuta-
neous reactions. In addition, individuals with sensitive 
skin are advised to clean their skin using gentle cleansers 
with a physiological pH, not to use too many cosmetics, 
and, in particular, to avoid fragranced products or those 
with preservatives that can sensitize the skin [2].
The results of our pre–post analysis of a large cohort of 
subjects has provided clear and valuable insights into 
the significant benefits of a postbiotic-based product for 
the management of sensitive skin in general practice, 
regardless of the type of factors initially involved in 
contributing to skin sensitivity. The analysis was based 
on self-assessment questionnaires, as well as objective 
and validated clinical assessment tools that were used in 
standardised conditions across participating centres and 
provided concordant and reliable findings. Overall, the 
study design allowed good-quality data to be collected 
for a large number of patients across multiple centres 
and countries, supporting the generalizability of the find-
ings. However, the real-world nature of our study led to 
some inherent limitations and did not allow a compar-
ison of the effects with a control vehicle or another 
soothing product. In addition, the possibility of reporting 
bias cannot be excluded, in particular for the assessment 
involving self-reported study product use.
Conclusion
This real-world international study demonstrated that 
the postbiotic ADE-G3based cream was an effective 
topical solution for sustainably soothing sensitive skin, 
calming skin discomfort and controlling skin hyper-re-
activity, regardless of the factors initially contributing to 
the skin sensitivity. The product was very well tolerated 
and most subjects reported high levels of satisfaction 
with the product. The product also allowed an immediate 
relief of symptoms and helped to improve QoL following 
regular use, which are key benefits for subjects living with 
this stressful and chronic syndrome. ■
